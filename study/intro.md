# Cancer

Uncontrolled growth of abnormal Cells -> Increase in the number of cells

Mutation: Something change in the genes of the DNA causing this abnormality

Two types of genes:
- Tumor suppressor gene: Stops cell division
- Proto oncogene: Promotes cell division

Mutations in any of these genes causes a tumor

## Types of Cancers

- Solid Cancers
  - Carcinomas: Occur in major organs
  - Sarcomas: Occur in connective tissues
- Blood cancers
  - Leukemias: Begin in bone marrow
  - Lymphomas: Begin in lymph nodes
 
## Risk Factors
- Age: Accumulation of mutations
- Cigarettes: Over 70 carcinogens
- Overweight: Increase of harmones and long-term inflamation
- Family history: 5%-10% are inherited
- Alcohol: Carcinogen by the national taxocology programme of US department of health and human services
- UV & Radiation


## Screening

Screening people based on risk factors to catch cancer before it gets worse

## Biopsy

Take a piece of the tumor and study it under a microscope by a pathologist
- Visualisation: Visual difference in the presented tumor
- Molecular tests: Checking for mutation gene


## Stages

Matastisation: Movement of cancer away from its point of origin

T: Tumor Size
N: Number of Lymph Nodes
M: Mastitisation

The increase in TNM increases the stage of the cancer from I II III IV
